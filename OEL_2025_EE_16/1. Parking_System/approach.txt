Parking System - Approach
==========================

This program manages a basic parking lot tracking vehicle types, calculating
charges, and reporting total revenue at the end of the session.

I used an infinite while(1) loop as the main driver, with a break on choice 4
(Exit) to end the session cleanly. This felt more natural than a do-while here
since the exit condition is menu-driven rather than a yes/no prompt.

The three vehicle rates (Car: Rs.100/hr, Bike: Rs.50/hr, Truck: Rs.200/hr) are
stored in named integer variables at the top rather than being magic numbers inside
the switch. This way, if the rates ever need updating, there's one place to change them.

The flow for each vehicle entry is:
  1. Display menu, read vehicle type
  2. Validate choice (reject anything outside 1–4)
  3. Read parking hours
  4. Use a switch-case to compute charge = rate * hours
  5. Show the charge and ask for confirmation

The confirmation step before finalizing is intentional — it lets the operator
correct a mistake (wrong vehicle type or hours) before it gets counted. Only on
'y' confirmation does the vehicle count and revenue tally get updated.

A second prompt asks whether to continue or exit, giving control back to the
operator between entries.

Finally, after the loop breaks, total vehicles parked and total revenue are printed
as a simple end-of-session summary.
